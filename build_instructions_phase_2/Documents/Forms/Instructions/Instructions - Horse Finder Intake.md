HORSE FINDER INTAKE INSTRUCTIONS

INTERNAL USE / APPLICATION BUILD REFERENCE

Purpose: This document explains how the Horse Finder Client Intake Form should be used, displayed, and converted into an engagement.

WHEN TO PRESENT THIS FORM

Present this form when:

☐ A client requests assistance locating a horse

☐ A client expresses interest in purchasing a horse but does not have a specific horse identified

☐ A client wants professional assistance narrowing available options

Do not use this form when the client only wants:

☐ A single horse evaluated

☐ Assistance with an already identified purchase

☐ General advice without an engagement

PRIMARY INFORMATION COLLECTED

The form should establish:

☑ Who the client is

☑ What type of horse they are seeking

☑ Intended discipline/use

☑ Rider experience

☑ Budget expectations

☑ Search requirements

☑ Requested services

REQUIRED DATABASE FIELDS

Minimum required before generating engagement:

Client Name

Client Contact Information

Discipline / Intended Use

Budget Range

Experience Level

Search Objective

CONTRACT GENERATION INPUTS

This intake should populate:

Horse Finder Agreement:

☑ Client information

☑ Search criteria

☑ Intended use

☑ General scope

☑ Client expectations

INTERNAL REVIEW ITEMS

Before creating engagement:

Confirm:

☐ Budget is realistic for requested horse profile

☐ Rider experience matches requested horse

☐ Search area is defined

☐ Additional services are identified

APPLICATION DISPLAY NOTES

Recommended interface:

Short initial form.

Expandable sections:

Horse Preferences

Budget

Experience

Additional Requirements

Avoid requiring unnecessary horse details when client is only beginning a search.

COMMON ERRORS TO AVOID

Do not:

☐ Promise a specific horse outcome

☐ Represent that a suitable horse will be found

☐ Treat search assistance as a guarantee of purchase
